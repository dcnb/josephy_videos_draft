Denise Bennett wants LGBTQ people throughout Idaho to tell their stories. Since 2017, Bennett has traveled across Idaho, recording interviews with members of the LGBTQ community who are willing to speak about their experience.

The National Endowment for the Humanities (NEH) thought her work so important that they granted her a $30,000 challenge grant. The challenge is to match that $30,000 with non-federal or -state grants and donations.

We Need Your Help!

The NEH funds are only made available if we match them. Anything you can spare will help us reach our goal for this project!

The "Idaho Queered" project can be supported by donations to the University of Idaho Foundation at 208-885-4000 or online UIdaho Giving by noting "Idaho’s LGBTQ Community Oral History Project" in the designation.

Join us for a preview of Denise's work. Selected clips from these histories will be shown together with Bennett's documentary on the murder of Steven Nelson, Gott's Point, at the Kenworthy Theatre in downtown Moscow, Idaho. The event will be by donation
